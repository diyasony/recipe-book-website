Ingredients (Serves 2-3)

  • 200g (7 oz) dried spaghetti
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (extra virgin recommended)
  • 3 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes or tomato puree
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste (optional for extra depth)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili flakes (optional)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish (optional)
  • Grated parmesan cheese to serve (optional but heavenly)

Instructions

  1. Boil the spaghetti: Bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil. Add spaghetti and cook according to package instructions (usually 8‚Äì10 minutes) until al dente. Drain and set aside, reserving 1/2 cup of pasta water.
  2. Make the garlic tomato sauce: In a wide pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped garlic and saut√© for about 1 minute until fragrant but not browned.
  3. Add tomato magic: Pour in the crushed tomatoes and tomato paste (if using). Stir well and let it simmer gently for 10‚Äì15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add oregano, chili flakes, salt, and pepper.
  4. Combine pasta & sauce: Add the drained spaghetti to the sauce and toss until evenly coated. If it seems too thick, add a splash of reserved pasta water to loosen it up.
  5. Serve like a pro: Plate it up while warm. Top with grated parmesan cheese and fresh basil if desired. Serve with garlic bread and a side of good vibes. üòå
